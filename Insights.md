# Key Insights
## Demand Drivers
- Inventory availability is the strongest determinant of realized sales.
- Products with insufficient stock experience lost sales even when demand exists.

## Promotion Effectiveness

- Promotions increase demand, but only when sufficient inventory is available.
- Running promotions without inventory preparation leads to stockouts and revenue loss.

## Category Demand Behavior

- Demand patterns vary significantly across categories.
- Certain categories show higher demand volatility, requiring larger safety stock buffers.

## Forecast Accuracy

- Machine learning forecasting achieved an average error of ~2.4 units, indicating strong predictive performance for retail demand estimation.

## Inventory Risk

- Inventory risk is primarily driven by demand variability rather than sales volume.
- Categories with higher demand volatility require more conservative inventory policies.

## Cost Tradeoff

- Simulation shows that lost sales from stockouts are often more expensive than holding additional inventory.

Therefore, maintaining moderate safety stock is economically justified.

## Strategic Takeaway

- Retail demand forecasting must be tightly integrated with inventory planning.

- Optimizing predictions alone is insufficient — businesses must ensure operational feasibility through appropriate safety stock and replenishment policies.
